Career

Lindsey started her career in acting at a very young age. She performed in various notable stage plays such as The Mousetrap at Northlight Theatre, Trevor at A Red Orchid Theatre, and Never the Bridesmaid. She was associated with the American labor union SAG-AFTRA (Screen Actors Guild – American Federation of Television and Radio Artists).

She appeared in various American TV series such as ‘General Hospital’ (1963), ‘Empire’ (2015), ‘American Housewife’ (2016), ‘Chicago Justice’ (2017), ‘The Ms. Pat Show’ (2021), and ‘Selena: The Series’ (2021).

She acted in a few American films such as ‘Pry Me Open’ (2017) and ‘Signal To Noise’ (2019). Apart from acting, she was featured in various TV commercials for the brands like Spectracide, Amazon Prime, Sears, TJ MAXX, and T-Mobile.

She was also a philanthropist and was working for the welfare of animals and human rights.

Death

On February 16, 2022, her missing report was filed by the Los Angeles Police Department. Her cousin Savannah Pearlman took to Twitter to ask for help from people in finding her. She tweeted,

LA people, my cousin – Lindsey Pearlman – is missing. Her phone last pinged on Sunset blvd. My uncle is offering a reward that leads to finding her, please keep your eyes open. #MissingPerson #LA.”

Lindsey Pearlman’s missing information

Two days later, her body was found in Hollywood, California, U.S. According to the local police,

Investigators had sought the public’s help in finding Lindsey, 43, who was last seen around noon last Sunday, ABC 7 reported. Her body was found Friday morning when officers responded to a call for a death investigation in a residential neighbourhood of Hollywood. The cause of death and the circumstances of her disappearance are under investigation.”

On her demise, her husband said,

The police found Lindsey. She’s gone. I’m broken. I will share more later, but I wanted to thank you to everyone for their love and efforts and ask you to respect the privacy of her family at this time.”

The American filmmaker shared a picture of Lindsey with her pet cat and pet dog on his Twitter account and wrote,

This is how I will remember Lindsey Pearlman. Effortlessly beautiful and talented. Hilarious. Compassionate. Unapologetic. She lit up every room. She listened intently on stage and off. She so loved animals. I am sorry to her family and all the communities who deeply adored her.”

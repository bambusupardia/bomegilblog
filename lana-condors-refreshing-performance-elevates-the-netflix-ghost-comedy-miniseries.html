Lana Condor's refreshing performance elevates the Netflix ghost comedy miniseries

August 30, 2024
post

The long-awaited ghost comedy miniseries Boo, Bitch started streaming on Netflix on July 8, 2022. Lana Condor, who is known for her roles in Moonshot and To All the Boys, plays the main character, Erika, in the quirky miniseries. Zoe Colletti, who is known for her role in Scary Stories to Tell in the Dark, plays the other main character, Gia.

The Netflix miniseries was directed by Kuba Soltysiak, Lauren Iungerich, Erin Ehrlich, and Tim Schauer. Jewel Chanel, Vivian Huang, Sonia Kharkar, and Stefanie Leder wrote the scripts.

Since the miniseries started to show up on the popular streaming service Netflix, it has been getting a lot of good reviews from both critics and viewers. The plot is fresh, quirky, and unique, and the two lead actresses do a great job. Lana Condor has gotten a lot of praise because she has taken the whole miniseries to the next level.

So, let’s not waste any more time and get right to finding out how the latest Netflix miniseries, Boo, Bitch, turned out.

Boo, Bitch

Review of the brand new Netflix comedy miniseries Boo, Bitch

A refreshing and quite quirky Gen Z teen comedy

The new Netflix miniseries Boo, Bitch is a comedy-drama about Gen Z teens that is told in a unique way. It tells the same old story of an invisible teen girl who becomes the most popular person in high school, but it does so in a completely new way that puts the emotional parts in the spotlight.

This is what makes the miniseries’ story different from others. The miniseries is made up with a lot of weird and over-the-top parts that make it a lot of fun to watch.

But the emotional dynamic and relationship between Erika and Gia, who are best friends, is what makes the miniseries stand out, especially in the last parts.

The writers of the latest Netflix comedy miniseries did a great job of showing how real the friendship between the two best friends is in a way that Gen Z would understand. So, the writing is quite good, and that helps the miniseries do well.

After being in Netflix’s To All the Boys series, Lana Condor has become a fan favorite. But in this brand-new Netflix miniseries, the actress broke Erika’s character arc, making the audience love her quirky and tough Erika. Seeing the actress in this way is a real breath of fresh air.

Lana’s character changes a lot over the course of the miniseries. She goes from being the shy, invisible girl to the sassy queen of high school to being her old, kind self.

It’s safe to say that the actress has hit a home run with her charming on-screen presence and fascinating acting performance. The way her character feels about Zoe Colletti’s character Gia is fascinating to watch.

Erika laying out Gia’s prom dress and realizing that Gia will never get to wear it is a heartbreaking scene that deserves to be called out.
